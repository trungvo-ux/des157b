Ratti, a professor at MIT and director of the Senseable City Lab, offers credible insights into urban sensing and citizen-based data systems. Pasquale examines the role of opaque algorithms in shaping societal structures, particularly in finance, law, and surveillance. This book is crucial to understanding the dangers of unaccountable data systems, directly aligning with the capstone’s themes of control and visibility. His background in law and technology strengthens the credibility of his critique. Pasquale's concept of "black box profiling" supports the speculative use of social ratings in the project. By using AI-generated mugshots, the project removes identifiable real-world data while still triggering an emotional reaction from users. Its realism adds narrative power to the speculative concept.
